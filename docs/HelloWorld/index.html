Hello World

We created a game called Hello World!

Hello World is a 2D platformer inspired by Super Mario Galaxy that was released in 2007 for the Nintendo Wii. What made this game feel and play unique would be the way the player wIn it, the player controls a large robot continuing to the last city of humanity. The robot is traveling through space, hopping between planets to find a new world to settle. On the way, the robot must battle deadly aliens.

The Game

Here is the game!

How to Play the game:
  • Spacebar / a / w to jump once
  • Press again for a double jump (the faster you press the higher you go!)
  • a / left arrow to move counter-clockwise
  • d / right arrow to move clockwise
  • Left mouse button to shoot
  • If you jump out of gravity, use bullets to propel yourself!

    Development Log of our game:

    Created by: Sage Walsh, Connor Goodman, Eric Trofimchik, and Mark Mavis

    Humanity is on the brink of extinction due to climate change. In a last ditch effort, the remaining survivors of Earth travel through the cosmos, searching for a new world to colonize. The transportation: A giant robot that hops between planets. Against incredibly dangerous odds, will the humans be able to say “Hello” to a new world?

  • Would you like the game to be harder, easier, or stay the same?
  • Which planet size is your favorite?
  • Which planet's gravity "feels" best?
  • Were there any planets you hated?
  • Did you like the coin placement?
  • Did you like the speed of the player?
  • Did you know the bullets could propel you when you are out of gravity?
  • Were there any parts of the game you found too difficult?
  • Were there any parts of the game you found too easy?

    • Session 1: Alpha

    Reviewers: 12

    The most common critiques were:
  • It is easy to die if an alien is right where you land
  • Green orbs are not clearly ammo
  • The ability to shoot through planets is not intuitive
  • Checkpoints would be a great addition
  • Shooting in zero gravity is not intuitive
  • Bullets do not clearly damage the enemies
  • It is not clear how to jump high enough to escape the planet
  • A progress bar would be helpful
  • The movement could be snappier
  • The game's single hit to die system is unforgiving
  • The coin placement should be closer to the planet in some cases
  • Enemy's collider could fit better
    • Changes as a result of the feedback:
  • Added a player health bar that takes 3 hits to die
  • Adjusted the enemy's collider
  • Added tutorial text to train the player of mechanics
  • Rebuilt the level making jumps easier
  • Fixed the coin placement
  • Added cracks in the enemy's sprite to indicate health

    • Session 2: Beta

    Reviewers: 6

    The most common critiques were:
  • The artwork is inconsistent
  • The music is too loud
  • The sound effects are too loud
  • There is no way to mute the sound
  • The tutorial text goes away too quickly
  • Player feels too slow
    • Changes made as a result of the feedback:
  • Sound and music levels were adjusted
  • A mute button was added
  • Tutorial text time was lengthened

    • Session 3: Final Playtest

    Reviewers: 10

    The most common critiques were:
  • The player feels too slow
  • The game could use a healing system
  • It is frustrating when you hit aliens while jumping between planets
  • The game could use an alien indicator
  • Double jump timing is not intuitive
  • More zero gravity sections would be fun
  • Larger planets and smalelr planets are not as fun as the medium ones
  • Aliens could be more difficult
  • Muted audio resets after player death
  • The shooting sound is too loud
  • The level is too long
  • The game is only one level

    • The first change in the final build of the game will be to address audio bugs. The second change will be increasing the player speed slightly. We have a lot of feedback saying the player should be faster. However, if they are too fast some of the game physics do not work as well. A third change in the final build of the game will be a healing system. Coins will slightly heal the player, making the game more forgiving and giving another reason to collect coins.

    The first future change we would like to extend the game with is more alien types. More aliens types, such as walking aliens and shooting aliens, would add more challenge and depth to the game. A second change we would like to make is redesigning the double jump. The current double jump is not intuitive to the player in how jump height works. Instead of activating the second jump at the apex of the first jump, the player must press jump for the second time immediately after the first. We would like to rework it so that the second jump must be timed with the apex of the first. A third change we would like to make is breaking up the game into multiple, shortwer levels. This way the player can save their progress more easily and lose less progress when they die. This would also make the game longer.

    Team Reflection

    One thing our team did very well was communicate clearly and consistently with each other. We always kept each other informed on who was doing what and when. To shop for this, we have a highly active discord. Another thing we did well was grant each other high levels of creative freedom. Whoever was inspired to do something in the game was highly encouraged to chase after that inspiration. To show for this, we have highly unique planets, music, player art, and level design. A third thing we did really well was adapt the game to challenging situations. We originally intended for the player to get sucked into the sun if out of a planet’s gravity. However, this proved to be clunky, buggy, and not fun. We swapped this feature out at a moment’s notice with an oxygen bar system that kills the player if they leave gravity.

    If we had a few more weeks for the game, the first priority would be to add health pickups. Currently, there is no way for the player to heal. This can be frustrating and difficult. Health pickups would address this. The second priority would be to add more levels. Our game is currently one long level. We would like to swap this with several slightly shorter levels. A third priority item would be to add new alien types. Currently, there is one alien type. The game would be deeper and more complex with more aliens that could be/ attack in different ways.
